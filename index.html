Honeypot Quarters

A trust of five woods, in five countries — kept in perpetuity since one thousand nine hundred and twenty-six.

Est. MCMXXVI

The trust owns five woodlands, each of one hundred acres, in five countries — and nothing else.

This is its atlas. There is a wood in Sussex, one in the Dordogne, one in Andalusia, one in Tuscany, and one on the northern slopes of the western Caucasus. Each is open, daylight hours, freely, to anyone who goes gently. Choose a wood on the map below, or read the charter we keep them under.

Choose a wood to enter

Each pin opens that wood’s page — its map, its features, its warden.

From the Sussex archive

The atlas, kept at the lodge.

The trust holds a single bound atlas, dated 1834, on a wooden lectern at the Sussex warden’s lodge. Each property was marked on its page on the day of acquisition, in the same pencil, by the warden on duty. The pencil has been replaced four times.

The Charter

The trust’s founding charter, written in 1926, is two pages long and has never been amended. It is set out in full, here, with the trust’s commentary.

Trustees & Wardens

Five trustees, five resident wardens. The full board meets in person on the first Saturday of November, at the Sussex lodge, and at no other time.

On Visiting

No charge, no booking, no map to download. Maps are kept at each warden’s lodge. Please go gently, and leave the wood as you found it.
