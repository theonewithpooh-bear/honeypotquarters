The Visitors’ Book

Excerpted entries from the five lodge books, May 2026. Reproduced with the visitors’ permission, save where they are anonymous.

A visitors’ book has been kept at each warden’s lodge since the wood was acquired. Entries are voluntary; we read them.

Sussex · The Hundred Acre Wood

2 May H. Treadwell First time. Saw a deer at the dell.
4 May M. & L. Patel Brought sandwiches; ate them at the bee tree. Pleasant.
9 May anonymous Returned after seven years. Still quiet.
12 May F. Owusu Walked the rope bridges. Wardens replacing the third one. Said hello.
17 May The Roberts family (4) Picnic. Hailstorm at 3 p.m. Lodge tea was extremely timely. Thank you.
24 May D. Konstantinos Beautiful. Will return.
26 May Polly (age 9) I saw the North Pole. It is a stump but that is alright.

Dordogne · Le Bois de Cent Acres

1 mai J. Mareuil Le sentier est sec. Les châtaigniers sont sortis. Bonne année.
6 May S. and T. Williams Walked from Sarlat. Forty-three minutes, and worth it.
11 May anonymous The bees were busy at the lime.
18 May B. Singh My third visit. The wall is still collapsed in the same places. I find this reassuring.
22 May Mme V. Dupin Le pas du pèlerin est intact. Comme il se doit.

Andalusia · El Bosque de las Cien Acres

3 May P. Edwards Hot. The cork trees do not seem to mind.
8 May anonymous I sat at the lynx stones for an hour. Nothing happened, which was the right thing.
14 mayo A. Ortega y familia Hemos vuelto a la fuente. El arroyo todavía corre, pero poco.
19 May L. Kobayashi Travelled a long way to see a wood. The wood was a wood. I am pleased.
28 May G. and the dog Sixteen miles in total. The dog is now asleep.

Tuscany · Il Bosco dei Cento Acri

5 maggio D. Russo Sono passato per la cappella. Sempre uguale. Buon così.
10 May N. Almeida A ruin in a wood. I have a new favourite kind of place.
15 May Mr and Mrs A. Lin Heard a porcupine in the brambles. Did not see it. This is correct.
21 May anonymous I had been told the chapel was “disappointing.” It is not.

Krasnodar · Сто-Акровый Лес

2 мая П. Волков Открыли ворота. Лес стоит. The gate is open.
7 May A. and N. Sarkisyan First visitors of the year, the warden said. He gave us tea.
13 May anonymous I came for the firs. I will come back for the firs.
20 May The Petrosyan family We left flowers at the 1944 stone, as we do every year.
27 May K. Reyes Caught in mist on the upper crest. The warden walked me down. Thank you, Pavel.

Each visitors’ book has been kept at its lodge in unbroken sequence since the wood was acquired. The Sussex book is the oldest, started in November 1926; the Krasnodar book is the newest, started 4 May 2005. All five books are kept open on a wooden stand by the lodge door. We do not transcribe them, save for these excerpts, twice a year.
