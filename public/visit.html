You are already welcome

On Visiting

No charge, no booking, no map to download. Maps, where they exist, are kept at each warden’s lodge.

Please go gently, follow the paths where they exist, and leave the wood as you found it. Or, if you must, better.

When the woods are open

The Hundred Acre Wood

Sussex, England

South-gate access by the warden’s lodge. Bicycles on the upper path only. Sunday teas at the lodge, three to five, between Easter and Michaelmas.

Open daily, dawn to dusk · year-round

Le Bois de Cent Acres

Dordogne, France

Reached on foot from Sarlat by the old pilgrim path, about forty minutes. No car park; the warden’s cottage stands half a mile north.

Open daily, dawn to dusk · year-round

El Bosque de las Cien Acres

Andalusia, Spain

Reached by the old shepherds’ path from the village. Water is scarce July to September; bring your own. Keep to marked tracks during October of the harvest year (next: 2027).

Open daily, dawn to dusk · year-round

Il Bosco dei Cento Acri

Tuscany, Italy

Reached on foot from the village by the pilgrim path, or from the western lane by a short cart-track. The warden is generally to be found at the wood between nine and noon.

Open daily, dawn to dusk · year-round

Сто-Акровый Лес

Krasnodar, Russia

Reached from Mezmay by a forestry road and then two kilometres on foot. The wood carries snow from late November; the May Gate opens for the season on the second of the month.

Open 2 May – 31 October · daylight hours

A few small rules

  1. Dogs are welcome on leads, in all five woods.
  2. Bicycles are permitted only on the upper path at Sussex and the May Gate path at Krasnodar; everywhere else, on foot only.
  3. If you find litter, please remove what you can.
  4. The bee tree at Sussex is not to be approached after July.
  5. The chapel at Tuscany is a ruin. Please do not try to make it less of one.
  6. The wartime memorial at Krasnodar is not the trust’s. Please leave it as you find it.
