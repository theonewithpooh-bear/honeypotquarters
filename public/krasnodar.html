Wood V.

Сто-Акровый Лес

Krasnodar Krai · The Western Caucasus

Caucasian beech and silver fir on the northern slopes of the western Caucasus; the oldest of the trust’s five woods by tree-age, with specimens recorded at more than four hundred years. The wood was also the trust’s longest acquisition: eleven years of negotiation, first letter to final deed.

The property is closed during the winter on the warden’s recommendation. The upper slopes carry snow from late November to the second of May, and the lower paths are impassable for most of that period. Lodge teas are served on alternate Sundays in summer, when the warden is in residence.

A small stone, not owned by the trust, stands at the eastern edge of the wood: a memorial set in 1944 by a local family for a son who did not return. The trust mows around it once a year and does not otherwise interfere.

“Лес стоит. Это всё, что от него требуется.”

— Mr P. Volkov, warden · “The wood stands. That is all that is asked of it.”

The wood is reached from the village of Mezmay by a forestry road and then on foot for the final two kilometres. The warden’s path, kept clear in summer, climbs about three hundred metres from gate to fir crest.

In this wood

  • The May Gate — opens for the season on the second of May.
  • The Beech Avenue — the wood’s most photographed feature.
  • The Fir Crest — silver fir on the upper slope; oldest specimens recorded.
  • The Wartime Memorial — not the trust’s; placed by a local family in 1944.
  • The Bear Stream — from the upper slope to the eastern boundary.
  • The Old Bridge — wooden, replaced every fifteen years.
  • The Warden’s Path — to the lodge; kept clear in summer.
